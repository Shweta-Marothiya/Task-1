SAVITRIBAI PHULE

About Savitribai Phule

Savitribai Phule (1831-1897) was a pioneering Indian social reformer, educator, and poet. She is renowned for her work in promoting education for women and marginalized communities in 19th-century India. Along with her husband, Jyotirao Phule, she established the first school for girls in Pune in 1848.

Savitribai Phule challenged the deeply entrenched caste and gender biases of her time. She advocated for the education of women and lower-caste individuals, which was considered radical in her era. Her efforts laid the foundation for many of the social reforms in India.

Achievements

  • Established the first girls' school in Pune (1848)
  • Worked tirelessly for the upliftment of women and lower-caste communities
  • Authored several poems and literary works advocating social reform
  • Co-founded the Satyashodhak Samaj, an organization aimed at social reform
